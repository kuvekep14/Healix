What is Vitality Age?

Your Vitality Age estimates your biological age based on real health data. A Vitality Age lower than your actual age means your body is performing younger than average for your age group.

When a metric is missing, its weight is redistributed across the others. The more data you connect, the more accurate your score becomes.

Log a Test
Record a benchmark result. Be consistent — same time of day, rested state.
How to do it
1. Find a flat 1-mile route — 4 laps on a standard track works best
2. Warm up for 5 minutes with light walking
3. Walk the mile as fast as you can without breaking into a jog
4. Start a timer when you begin and stop it when you finish
5. Immediately check your heart rate at the finish line — count your pulse for 15 seconds and multiply by 4
6. Enter your results below
Tip: Wear a heart rate monitor for a more accurate finish HR. Convert your time to decimal minutes (e.g. 14 min 30 sec = 14.5).
How to do it
1. Go to a standard track (each lap = 400m) or use a GPS watch on a flat route
2. Warm up for 5-10 minutes with light jogging
3. Set a timer for exactly 12 minutes
4. Run as far as you can — pace yourself so you can sustain effort the full 12 minutes
5. When the timer goes off, stop and note your total distance
6. Count your laps and any partial distance — e.g. 6 laps = 2400m
Tip: Most people start too fast. A steady pace where you're breathing hard but not sprinting will give you the best result.
